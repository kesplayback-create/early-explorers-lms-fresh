Lesson 1 · Why Policies Matter
Policies protect everyone — children, staff, and the setting

⚖️

Guidance — When unsure, policies provide a clear framework for decision-making

Daily Procedures & Protocols

Procedures turn policies into action. Learn the step-by-step processes you'll follow every day.

📽 4 slides⏱ ~7 minutes✅ 1 knowledge check
Lesson 2 · Opening & Closing Procedures
Start and end of day procedures
☀️
Opening (7:00 AM)
Unlock, disarm alarm, complete safety check, turn on heating/lights, check registers, set up rooms
👋
Drop-off (7:30-9:30 AM)
Greet families warmly, sign children in, note any messages from parents, conduct visual health check
🍽️
Meal & Snack Times
Handwashing before and after, allergy awareness, supervise eating, record food intake for babies
🚪
Collection (3:00-6:00 PM)
Only release to authorized adults, share key info about the child's day, sign children out
🌙
Closing (6:00 PM)
All children collected, tidy and clean, complete daily sheets, lock up, set alarm
🔐
Security Protocol
Never prop doors open. All visitors must sign in and wear a visitor badge. Challenge anyone you don't recognize.

Your Professional Responsibilities

As an Early Years practitioner, you have legal and professional duties beyond just caring for children. This lesson clarifies what's expected of you.

📽 3 slides⏱ ~6 minutes🏆 Module complete
Lesson 3 · Professional Standards
You are expected to uphold these standards at all times
🤝
Maintain professional boundaries — Friendly but not friends. No personal social media contact with parents
🔒
Confidentiality — Never discuss children or families outside work. No photos of children on personal devices
👔
Professional appearance — Clean, appropriate clothing. Hair tied back. Minimal jewelry. Closed-toe shoes
📚
Continuous development — Engage in training, reflective practice, and supervision
⏰
Punctuality & reliability — Arrive on time. Notify manager if sick or late. No unauthorized absences
⚠️
Gross Misconduct
Breaching confidentiality, inappropriate relationships with families, dishonesty, and safeguarding failures can result in immediate dismissal.
