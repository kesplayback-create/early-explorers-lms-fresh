Fire Safety at Early Explorers

Fire safety is everyone's responsibility. Learn our evacuation procedures, alarm points, assembly areas, and your duties in an emergency.

📽 5 slides⏱ ~6 minutes✅ 1 knowledge check
Lesson 1 · Fire Alarm & Evacuation
When the fire alarm sounds
1
STOP what you are doing immediately
Do not collect belongings. Grab the register/roll call list ONLY.
2
Calmly gather your group of children
Reassure them. Use a calm voice. Walk, do not run.
3
Exit via the nearest safe fire exit
At Early Explorers: Baby room → rear garden exit. Toddler & Preschool → front exit.
4
Go directly to the assembly point
Assembly point: Far corner of front garden by the gate (weather permitting) or across the road at the park gates.
5
Conduct roll call immediately
Report to the Fire Warden (Manager). Do not re-enter the building.
🚨
Never Assume It's a Drill
Every alarm must be treated as real. Lives depend on immediate, calm action.
⚠️
NEVER use the lift during a fire evacuation. NEVER go back inside for belongings or missing items.

Accident & Incident Reporting

Accurate recording protects children, staff, and the setting. Learn what to record, when, and how to complete accident forms correctly.

📽 4 slides⏱ ~6 minutes✅ 1 knowledge check
Lesson 2 · What Must Be Recorded
All accidents and incidents must be documented
🩹
Any injury to a child — bumps, grazes, cuts, bites, falls — no matter how minor
🚨
Head injuries — ALWAYS notify parents immediately, even if the child seems fine
⚡
Near-misses — incidents that could have caused harm (broken equipment, hazards, close calls)
🩺
Staff injuries — any injury sustained at work, even minor, must be logged
📝
Record accidents on the SAME DAY they occur. Delayed reporting can be seen as a safeguarding concern during Ofsted inspections.
📖
The Accident Book
Located in the office. Every entry must be signed by the staff member who witnessed the accident AND by the parent at collection.
🧠 Knowledge Check · Lesson 2

B
Record the injury immediately, notify parents, and monitor closely
C
Wait until collection to mention it to the parent
D
Don't record it if the child is fine

First Aid Procedures

Knowing who the first aiders are and when to call them can save lives. Learn the basics and when to escalate.

📽 3 slides⏱ ~5 minutes✅ 1 knowledge check
Lesson 3 · First Aiders at Early Explorers
Our paediatric first aiders:
👨‍⚕️
[Manager Name] — Primary first aider, qualified Paediatric First Aid
👩‍⚕️
[Deputy Name] — Secondary first aider, qualified Paediatric First Aid
🩹
First aid boxes — Located in each room + main office. Check monthly.
When to call a first aider:
🚨
Any bleeding that doesn't stop with direct pressure
😵
Loss of consciousness, seizure, or difficulty breathing
🤕
Head injury with vomiting, drowsiness, or confusion
📞
When to Call 999
Unconscious child, severe bleeding, choking, suspected broken bone, allergic reaction (anaphylaxis). Do NOT hesitate.

Hygiene & Infection Control

Preventing the spread of illness protects everyone. Learn our handwashing standards, cleaning protocols, and exclusion periods.

📽 4 slides⏱ ~6 minutes✅ 1 knowledge check
Lesson 4 · Handwashing Protocol
When to wash hands (staff and children):
🚪
On arrival at nursery — before touching anything or anyone
🍽️
Before and after eating — including snacks and meals
🧷
After nappy changes or toileting — every single time, no exceptions
🤧
After wiping noses, coughing, sneezing
🧸
After messy play or outdoor play
Proper technique: Wet hands → Soap → Rub all surfaces for 20 seconds → Rinse → Dry with paper towel
🦠
Illnesses Spread Fast
In group childcare, one unwashed hand can spread illness to 15+ children within hours. Handwashing is THE most effective infection control measure.

Risk Assessments & Daily Checks

Prevention is better than cure. Learn what to check daily, how to conduct risk assessments, and when to raise concerns.

📽 4 slides⏱ ~7 minutes🏆 Module complete
Lesson 5 · Daily Safety Checks
Every morning before children arrive:
✓
Check your room for hazards
Broken toys, sharp edges, loose furniture, trip hazards, spillages
✓
Inspect outdoor play area
Broken glass, animal waste, water puddles, broken equipment
✓
Test fire exits are clear and unlocked
Never prop open fire doors or block exit routes
✓
Confirm first aid box is stocked
Plasters, sterile wipes, disposable gloves, emergency contact list
✓
Check registers and emergency contacts are updated
Ensure all children's records are current and accessible
👁️
See Something? Say Something
If you spot a hazard, broken equipment, or safety concern — report it immediately to your manager. Do not wait.
