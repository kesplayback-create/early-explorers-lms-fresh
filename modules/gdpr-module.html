GDPR & Confidentiality

Understanding your legal duties around data protection and confidentiality in early years settings.

📽 5 slides ⏱ ~8 minutes 🔒 Your legal duty
Lesson 1 · What is GDPR
GDPR: The law that protects personal information

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a UK law that controls how organisations collect, store, use and share personal data. At Early Explorers, we hold sensitive information about children and families — GDPR tells us exactly how to handle it responsibly.

2018
UK GDPR came into force
£17.5M
Maximum fine for breaches
72hrs
To report a data breach
🛡️
Personal Data at Early Explorers
We hold children's names, dates of birth, medical needs, family contacts, observations, and photos. All of this is personal data protected by GDPR.
Lesson 1 · Personal Data
What counts as personal data?
👤
Basic identifiers — names, addresses, dates of birth, phone numbers
🏥
Special category data — health information, allergies, medical conditions (extra protection required)
📸
Photos & videos — images of children are personal data and need parental consent
📋
Observation records — developmental notes, Learning Journals, EYFS assessments
👨‍👩‍👧
Family information — custody arrangements, contact restrictions, court orders
ℹ️
Special category data (health, ethnicity, religion) requires extra safeguards. Always treat it with the highest level of care and never share it without clear authorisation.
⚠️
Even casual conversations about a child to an unauthorised person can be a GDPR breach. Be mindful of who can hear you at all times.
Lesson 1 · The 7 Principles
GDPR is built on 7 principles
01
Lawful & Fair
Only process data with a legal basis and in a fair way
02
Purpose Limitation
Only use data for the reason it was collected
03
Data Minimisation
Only collect what you actually need
04
Accuracy
Keep records up to date and correct
05
Storage Limitation
Don't keep data longer than necessary
06
Security
Protect data from loss, theft or unauthorised access
⚖️
Accountability
The 7th principle — Early Explorers must be able to demonstrate compliance with all other principles. This is why we have a Data Protection Policy.

Your Responsibilities

What you must do every day to protect personal data and maintain confidentiality at Early Explorers.

📽 5 slides ⏱ ~7 minutes ✅ Daily practice
Lesson 2 · Daily Duties
Your day-to-day data protection duties
1
Keep records secure
Never leave children's files, registers or medical forms where unauthorised people can see them. Lock away or close down when not in use.
2
Passwords & screens
Lock your screen when stepping away. Never share passwords. Do not use personal devices to photograph or record children.
3
Conversations
Discuss children only with those who need to know. Never talk about families in the carpark, corridors or social settings.
4
Social media
Never post photos of children or share any information about families online — even in private groups. This is gross misconduct.
🚫
Posting photos of children on social media — even privately — is a serious data breach and gross misconduct. It can result in immediate dismissal.
📱
Personal Devices
Personal phones must not be used to photograph children. All photos must be taken on setting devices only with parental consent on file.
Lesson 2 · Sharing Data
When can you share information?
✅
With consent — the parent or carer has given written permission for that specific sharing
✅
Need to know — sharing within the team where it is necessary for the child's care
✅
Legal obligation — sharing with Ofsted, local authority or police when legally required
✅
Safeguarding — when a child is at risk, safeguarding overrides confidentiality. Share with DSL immediately.
❌
Never share with friends, other parents, family members, or on social media — regardless of context
💡
Safeguarding always takes priority over confidentiality. If a child is at risk, you must share information with the DSL even without parental consent.
🤝
Information Requests
If you are unsure whether you can share information, always check with your manager first. When in doubt, do not share.
Lesson 2 · Data Breaches
What is a data breach and what do you do?

A data breach is any incident where personal data is accessed, lost, altered or shared without authorisation. Examples include: leaving a child's file in a public area, sending an email to the wrong person, or a lost USB stick containing records.

1
Don't panic — act immediately
Stop the breach if possible (retrieve the document, close the email thread)
2
Report to your manager
Tell your manager straight away — even if you think it's minor
3
72-hour window
Serious breaches must be reported to the ICO within 72 hours — your manager handles this
⏱️
The ICO (Information Commissioner's Office) must be notified of serious breaches within 72 hours. Prompt reporting protects the setting and the families involved.
📋
Always Report
Never try to handle a breach alone. Report to your manager immediately — no matter how small it seems. Unreported breaches can become much bigger problems.

Rights & Our Practice

Understanding individual rights under GDPR and how Early Explorers embeds data protection into everyday practice.

📽 5 slides ⏱ ~5 minutes 🏆 Module complete
Lesson 3 · Individual Rights
Families have rights over their data
👁️
Right to Access
Parents can request to see all data held on their child
✏️
Right to Rectify
They can ask us to correct inaccurate information
🗑️
Right to Erasure
They can request deletion in certain circumstances
🚫
Right to Object
They can object to certain types of processing
📦
Portability
They can request their data in a portable format
⚙️
Restrict Processing
They can limit how we use their data
💡
If a parent makes a Subject Access Request (asking to see their data), refer them to the manager. We have 30 days to respond. Never ignore these requests.
Lesson 3 · Our Practice
How Early Explorers protects data
📝
Privacy notices — parents are informed at enrolment about what data we hold and why
🔐
Secure storage — physical files locked, digital data password protected, limited access
📸
Photo consent — written consent obtained before any photos are taken or shared
⏳
Retention periods — records kept only as long as required by law, then securely destroyed
🎓
Staff training — all staff complete GDPR training (you're doing it now!)
🗂️
Record Retention
Children's records are kept for a set period after they leave — typically until they turn 21 for safeguarding records. Never dispose of records without manager approval.
